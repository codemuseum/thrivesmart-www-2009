YouBook Privacy Policy

February 4, 2007

The ThriveSmart Privacy Policy describes how we treat personal information when you use ThriveSmart's products and services. In addition, the following describes our privacy practices that are specific to ThriveSmart YouBook ("YouBook").

Information we collect

  • Registration information - When you sign up for YouBook we ask for your personal information so that we can provide you with the service. The information we require to register for the service includes your full name and email address. For businesses, we also require you to provide your your address, your business category, and certain information about your appointment types and appointment schedule. This information allows us to process appointments and protect users from fraud. In some cases, we may also ask you to send us additional information or to answer additional questions to help verify your information. The information we collect is stored in association with your ThriveSmart Account.
  • Information obtained from third parties - In order to protect you from fraud or other misconduct, we may obtain information about you from third parties to verify the information you provide. Also, for businesses, we may obtain information about you and your business from a business information service such as Dun & Bradstreet.
  • Appointment information - When you use YouBook to conduct an appointment transaction, we collect information for and about each appointment, including the appointment amount, a description provided by the seller of type of appointment being reserved, the names of the business and end user, and additional information requested by the business in order for the end user to book the appointment.

How we use the information we collect

We will not sell or rent your personal information to companies or individuals outside of ThriveSmart. In addition, we will only use or share your personal information with other companies or individuals outside of ThriveSmart as described in the ThriveSmart Privacy Policy and in the following limited circumstances:

  • As necessary to process your appointment and maintain your account. Each business may require the end user to input particular person information, which is shared with the business. This will show up as form fields on the reservation web pages.
  • To detect, prevent, or otherwise address fraud, security or technical issues,
  • We may share aggregated non-personally identifiable information with third parties.. However, if we do share this aggregated information, we do not include personally identifiable information without your explicit opt-in consent or in the limited circumstances described above.

Information security

We take appropriate security measures to protect against unauthorized access to or unauthorized alteration, disclosure or destruction of data. These include internal reviews of our data collection, storage and processing practices and security measures, as well as physical and technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to systems where we store personal data. For example, your account password will always be stored in an encrypted form (except if you provide it to us in a communication outside of our web page interface).

The security of your account also depends on keeping your account password confidential, and you should not share your account name or password with anyone. If you do share your account information with a third party, they will have access to your account and your personal information.

For more information about our security practices, please see the main ThriveSmart Privacy Policy.

Accessing and updating personal information

You can review and update your appointment information by logging in to your ThriveSmart Account and going to the "Appointment History" page. You can also view your previous appointment history on the "Appointment History" page.

You can disable YouBook by contacting us. If you do so, your appointment information and transaction history will no longer be viewable through YouBook. However, in order to meet our reporting and auditing obligations, and to detect, deter, and prevent fraud or other misconduct on our systems, the information will be retained in our systems. If you disable YouBook, your personally identifiable information will not be used by ThriveSmart or shared with third parties except for these purposes. We may delete these records over time if permitted or required by law.

Disabling YouBook does not close or cancel your ThriveSmart Account. This Privacy Policy continues to apply to the personal information we maintain after you disable YouBook.

Web Beacons

Some of business that use YouBook use "web beacons" for the purposes of analyzing or auditing their website or appointment reservations. A web beacon is an electronic image in a web page, usually consisting of a single-pixel (1 x 1 GIF), that allows the website to analyze user traffic.

At the request of the merchant, we may serve web beacons on the business' behalf so that the merchant can analyze or audit their web traffic. We do not provide any personal information to merchants when serving web beacons on their behalf. In addition, we will provide you with a notice when a web beacon is served within YouBook.

Changes to this policy

Please note that this Privacy Policy may change from time to time. We will not reduce your rights under this Policy without your explicit consent. We will post any Policy changes on this page and, if the changes are significant, we will provide a more prominent notice (including notification in your account interface or email notification). Each version of this Policy will be identified at the top of the page by its effective date, and we will also keep prior versions of this Privacy Policy in an archive for your review.

If you have any questions about this Policy, please feel free to contact us through our website or write to us at Privacy Matters, c/o ThriveSmart, LLC, 2425 B Channing Way #462, Berkeley, California, 94704 USA.
